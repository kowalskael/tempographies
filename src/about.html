About
Considering the targets of EUGD for a 20% reduction of chemical fertilizers by 2030, it is essential to optimize nutrient use and recovery.
The project will provide a scientific cooperation framework among different disciplines targeting the process of developing a novel fertilizer from the raw waste material to a final application. It will also explore social aspects of scientific practice, human/non-human actors and interactions between them. Creation of a specially designed digital media environment will help to visualize crucial temporal, spatial, ecological, and cultural transformations involved in the process of trans-European scientific collaboration. It will combine the knowledge of the five ECRs involved in the thematic of biowastes processing (Stuttgart), materials functionalization (UHA), environmental analysis (AUTh), art and science research (AMU) and culture and technology studies (AMU).
